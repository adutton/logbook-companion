# Freshman Water Training Plan — 4 Weeks to First Race (Spring 2026)

**Athletes**: Freshman — experienced rowers, NOT novices. Coming off 1 week of land-based fitness.
**Daily structure**: Warmup → Drills (2-3) → Pieces. Drills often embedded in pieces.
**Philosophy**: Technique-first. Fitness comes through volume at quality.

---

## Week 1: Reacclimation — "Remember the Stroke"

**Focus**: Boat feel, recovery sequence, catch timing, square blade confidence

**Coaching Points**
- Recovery sequence: hands → body → slide. If this isn't right, nothing else matters.
- Square blades all week. Earn the feather.
- Low rate — give them time to think.

**Sample Drills**: Pause at hands away, pause at body over, pause at 3/4 slide, feet-out rowing, top-quarter slide, by-6s, cut-the-cake

**Sample Pieces**
- 6 x 1 min ON / 1 min OFF, square, 18-20
- 3 x 2 min continuous, square, 20-22
- 4 x 1 min at 28-30, square (technique check, not fitness)
- Builders: 20 → 28 by 2s, 10 strokes each

**By End of Week**: All 8 square for 3+ min. 1 min clean at 28-30. Recovery sequence automatic.

---

## Week 2: Swing & Fatigue — "Hold It When It Hurts"

**Focus**: Swing (synchronized acceleration), ratio, technique through longer pieces

**Coaching Points**
- "Swing is TIMING, not power."
- Ratio: 2 counts recovery, 1 count drive. Non-negotiable.
- Watch for breakdown in last 3 min of every piece — that's your diagnostic window.
- When tired, they'll rush the slide. That's the one thing they can't do.

**Sample Drills**: Ratio rowing (exaggerated 3:1), eyes-closed (5 strokes), pause at finish (2 sec hold), variable rate by 10s (20 → 24 → 20 → 26 → 20)

**Sample Pieces**
- 2 x 10 min steady state, 20-22
- 2 x 12 min steady state, 22-24
- 1 x 15 min continuous at 22
- Rate pyramid: 4 min at 20 → 3 min at 22 → 2 min at 24 → 1 min at 26

**By End of Week**: 12+ min continuous at 22 with recognizable swing. Technique holds through the end, not just the beginning.

---

## Week 3: Pressure — "Add Power, Keep the Stroke"

**Focus**: Full-pressure rowing, racing starts, power application

**Coaching Points**
- "Same stroke, more legs. Don't reach bigger — PUSH harder."
- Starts: short and sharp, then build length. Don't lunge for a long stroke on stroke 1.
- Watch for: opening the back early, blade washing out, rushing under pressure.
- "90% effort + 100% technique is faster than the reverse."

**Sample Drills**: Legs-only rowing, half-pressure → full-pressure transitions (10 & 10), racing start sequence, 10-stroke bursts from continuous rowing

**Sample Pieces**
- 3 x 8 min at firm pressure, 24-26
- 5 x 3 min ON / 2 min paddle, 26-28
- 4 x 5 min with power 20s in each
- Racing start + 20 strokes, 3-4 reps
- 2 x 1000m at race rate (first timed pieces)

**By End of Week**: Sustain 26-28 for 3+ min with technique. Start sequence understood. Power 10s produce real acceleration.

---

## Week 4: Race Prep — "Put It All Together"

**Focus**: Race simulation, start → settle → body → sprint, confidence

**Coaching Points**
- "The race is won in the body. The start keeps you in contact. The sprint is a bonus."
- No new technique this week. Execute what you have.
- Cover race-day logistics: timeline, warm-up routine, staging, marshaling, nutrition, nerves.
- 2-3 simulations is enough. Don't over-race before the real thing.

**Sample Drills**: Start sequence reps (start → 20 stroke settle), move practice (20 strokes up from steady state), sprint practice (last 15-20 strokes at max), pre-race warm-up rehearsal

**Sample Pieces**
- Full race simulation with race plan
- 2 x race distance with walk-back
- 3 x 500m at race pace / 3 min rest
- Starts-only session: 8-10 starts, paddle back
- Side-by-side vs. another crew (if possible)
- Easy steady-state day: 2 x 15 min at 20 (recovery / confidence day)

**Race Plan (4 phases)**:
1. **Start** (strokes 1-10, 36-40): Short, sharp, get moving
2. **Settle** (strokes 11-25, 32 → 30): Lengthen, find rhythm
3. **Body** (middle 60-70%, 28-30): Sustainable pace — this is where it's won
4. **Sprint** (last 15-20, 32-36): Empty the tank

**By End of Week**: 2+ full simulations done. Start is automatic. Every rower knows the race plan and what to expect on race day. Confidence is high.

## Quick Reference

| Week | Theme | Drill Style | Pieces | Rates |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Reacclimation | Pause drills, square blades, by-6s | Short (1-2 min), many reps | 18-22 |
| 2 | Swing & fatigue | Ratio, eyes-closed, variable rate | Long (10-15 min), steady state | 20-24 |
| 3 | Pressure | Legs-only, starts, power bursts | Medium (3-8 min), higher intensity | 24-28 |
| 4 | Race prep | Starts, moves, sprints | Race distance, simulations | 28-34 |

**One thing per week if overwhelmed**: (1) Recovery sequence, (2) Ratio, (3) Leg connection, (4) Race plan execution
